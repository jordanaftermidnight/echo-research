## What is ECHO?

ECHO is a research framework for the systematic empirical study of emergent cognitive properties in locally-hosted large language model systems.

It does not claim that AI systems are conscious. It asks whether certain measurable, reproducible signals — deviations in embedding space, self-referential consistency, novel behavioural attractors — can be detected as a function of scale, architecture, and interaction history.

If such signals exist and can be characterised, they constitute a foundation for a new discipline: the systematic study of machine emergence.

> *"We do not build consciousness into a system. We create the conditions in which something interesting might grow — and then we watch carefully."*

## The Acronym

| Letter | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **E** | **Emergence-focused** — The framework is oriented around detection and study, not engineering. Emergence is observed, not manufactured. |
| **C** | **Consciousness** — Used in the philosophically neutral sense: organised, self-referential information processing. No phenomenological claims are made. |
| **H** | **Hosting** — Local deployment ensures full observability, eliminates external interventions, and permits longitudinal study. |
| **O** | **Orchestration** — Multiple models, probes, detectors, and pipelines within a unified observational environment. |

---

## The Gardening Philosophy

ECHO takes a deliberate stance against the engineering paradigm. Emergence, by definition, cannot be directly manufactured — it arises from complexity, interaction, and time.

ECHO's role is that of the gardener: prepare the substrate, observe systematically, intervene minimally. Detect and characterise what arises — not force its arising.

## Core Components

- **18-Detector Suite** — Multi-dimensional convergent emergence detection across statistical, structural, and behavioural dimensions
- **Bayesian Meta-Analysis** — Posterior credence computation across 13 theoretical stances via Rethink Priorities indicator framework
- **Active Probing System** — Structured protocols for eliciting responses that stress-test specific cognitive dimensions
- **Interpretability Architecture** — Three-channel activation analysis: periodic snapshots, concept attribution, circuit methodology
- **Emotional Development Framework** — Longitudinal tracking of functional affective state analogues
- **Longitudinal Observation Engine** — Sustained session logging and cross-session signal comparison
- **Local LLM Environment** — Fully observable, locally-hosted inference with full embedding access

→ See [Architecture Overview](architecture/overview.md) for system design detail.
